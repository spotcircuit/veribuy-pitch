My Approach

What the system actually does

Four capabilities that turn VeriBuy from a tool into a compounding asset.

Capture & Structure

Inspector uploads 10 photos of a vehicle. The system instantly validates quality, flags blurry shots, extracts location data, and stores everything in a format ready for analysis. No manual cleanup needed.

Score & Explain

Instead of a black-box number, clients get a breakdown: "Body panels: 6.2 — moderate dent detected on rear quarter panel." Every score backed by visual evidence.

Learn & Improve

A lender denies a loan. That outcome flows back to VeriBuy and teaches the model what a "bad score" actually looks like in the real world. Accuracy compounds with every decision.

Track Over Time

Same car inspected in January at 87, comes back in July at 62. VeriBuy sees the delta — flags deterioration, catches fraud. No single-inspection tool can do this.

Architecture Decisions

Why it's built this way

Every technical decision maps to a business outcome.

Structured from day one

Business: When you're ready to train your own model, every past inspection is already usable. No re-collecting data. No lost history.

Technical: Normalized storage, quality signals, embeddings computed at ingest.

Quality-aware scoring

Business: A blurry photo doesn't produce a bad score — it gets down-weighted. Clients trust the output even when inspectors aren't perfect.

Technical: Blur, brightness, contrast signals weight each image's contribution.

Gradual AI independence

Business: You keep using OpenAI today while quietly building your own model. Zero client disruption. Switch when the internal model proves itself.

Technical: Shadow mode → canary routing → graduated rollout.

Asset history = moat

Business: A competitor can copy your model. They can't copy 2 years of condition history on 50,000 vehicles. That data advantage is permanent.

Technical: Asset graph links inspections per VIN/policy over time.

Intelligence Layer

How the system gets smarter

Most platforms score and forget. VeriBuy captures three signals that compound over time.

Expert Labels

Domain experts review inspections and score six dimensions — paint, panels, glass, wheels, interior, mechanical. This teaches the model what "good" and "bad" actually look like.

Technical: Label Studio with 6-dimension taxonomy, inter-rater agreement tracking, dual annotation on 20%.

Real-World Outcomes

A lender approves a loan. Six months later, the borrower defaults. That outcome flows back and tells VeriBuy: "your score was too high." This is the signal competitors don't have.

Technical: Outcome capture API, materialized training view joining scores to decisions.

Corrections

When a human reviewer disagrees with a score, that correction is captured and fed back. The rate of corrections decreasing over time is how you measure the model improving.

Technical: Label correction pipeline, correction rate as key model health metric.

AI Independence

Owning your intelligence — without risk

You don't flip a switch from external API to internal model. You earn it in three stages.

Shadow

What clients see: Nothing changes. Same API, same results.

What happens behind the scenes: Your internal model runs on every request silently. You compare its answers to the external API and measure the gap.

Canary

What clients see: Still nothing. Identical experience.

Behind the scenes: 10% of requests use the internal model when it's confident. If anything looks off, you pull back instantly.

Graduate

The result: Internal model handles most traffic. External API stays as safety net. API costs drop. You own the intelligence.

Built on a proven vision model adapted to your domain — not trained from scratch.

Client Output

What the client actually receives

Not a black-box number. A full condition report they can defend to regulators, underwriters, and buyers.

Condition Breakdown

A composite score (1–100) broken into dimensions: "Body panels: 6.2 — dent on rear quarter." Each dimension weighted by how much it affects value. Structural issues tank the score; cosmetic ones don't.

Visual Evidence

Each finding links to the actual photo with the problem area highlighted. The client can see exactly what the AI saw and why it scored that way. No guessing.

Coverage Report

The report shows what was photographed and what wasn't. "Front, rear, both sides captured. Engine bay and underbody: missing." The client knows the gaps before making a decision.

Audit Trail

Which model version scored this. What confidence level. Whether it was routed internally or through the external API. Full traceability for regulated industries.

Real-World Challenges

The hard parts — and how I'd solve them

Inspectors aren't perfect

Reality: Photos will be blurry, poorly lit, missing angles. You can't control field conditions.

Solution: The system detects quality issues automatically and down-weights unreliable images instead of producing unreliable scores.

Clients need it yesterday

Reality: Dealers and auction houses need turnaround in minutes, not days.

Solution: The pipeline processes photos as they're uploaded. By the time the last photo lands, the first ones are already scored.

Every client integrates differently

Reality: Insurers want PDF reports. Lenders want API calls. Marketplaces want embedded widgets.

Solution: One scoring engine, multiple output formats. JSON API, PDF generation, webhook callbacks. No custom builds per client.

Regulation & trust

Reality: Insurers can't use a tool they can't explain to regulators.

Solution: Every score decomposed into weighted dimensions with evidence. Full audit trail. The math is visible.

Foundation

Building on what already works

I wouldn't rewrite your stack. I'd extend it. Every addition has a clear reason.

Python backend stays

What I'd add: A FastAPI service for the model. Same language your team already knows. Async, production-grade, easy to deploy alongside what exists.

Supabase stays

What I'd add: The pgvector extension for image embeddings. It lives inside your existing Postgres — no new database, no new vendor, no migration.

OpenAI stays (for now)

What I'd add: An inference router in front of it. Today it passes everything through. Tomorrow it starts routing to your own model. Zero disruption.

Cloud hosting stays

What I'd add: Structured S3 storage for media and a message queue for the processing pipeline. This is how inspection data becomes training data.

Cold Start

Bootstrapping the data advantage

The model needs data before it can learn. Three paths running in parallel to get there fast.

Label existing data

Every inspection from the current prototype gets run through the labeling tool. Two domain experts, a focused sprint, and you have over a thousand labeled images from data you already own.

Multiply what you have

Each labeled photo generates multiple training variants — different lighting, angles, compression. The AI sees more diversity without anyone taking more photos.

Partner for outcomes

Offer a regional insurer or lender free condition scoring on their historical backlog. In return, they share their decisions. Those real-world outcomes are 10x more valuable than manual labels.

The partner path is also a sales pipeline — you're demonstrating VeriBuy's value while building the dataset that makes it better.
